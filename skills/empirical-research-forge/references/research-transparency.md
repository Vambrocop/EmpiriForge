# Research Transparency

Use this reference when the task involves preregistration, deviations, data provenance, code disclosure, restricted data, AI assistance, or open-science norms.

## Transparency Ledger

Record:

- research question and hypotheses;
- whether the analysis was preregistered;
- deviations from preregistration or pre-analysis plan;
- data sources, access dates, licenses, and restrictions;
- sample exclusions and construction rules;
- outcome and treatment definitions;
- code entry point and environment;
- stochastic steps, seeds, and package versions;
- AI assistance in coding, writing, extraction, or review;
- materials that cannot be shared and why.

## Preregistration and Deviations

Do not pretend an exploratory analysis was confirmatory.

Use clear labels:

- `pre-specified`: defined before outcome inspection.
- `deviation`: changed from a plan, with reason.
- `exploratory`: useful but not confirmatory.
- `robustness`: checks a named threat.
- `mechanism`: tests a pathway, not the main effect itself.

## Restricted Data

If data cannot be shared:

- name the restriction type: legal, contractual, privacy, IRB, commercial, or platform terms;
- provide access instructions when possible;
- provide code and documentation even when raw data cannot be redistributed;
- document which outputs can be reproduced by outsiders;
- avoid fake openness: do not imply full reproducibility when access is limited.

## AI Assistance Disclosure

When AI materially affects the project, document:

- which tasks used AI;
- whether AI wrote or modified code;
- whether AI summarized literature or drafted text;
- whether human review checked citations, code, and claims;
- whether any generated content remains in the final paper.

Follow the target journal, university, or funder policy when one exists.
